You do not need a fermentation chamber or a hydrometer to start. You almost
certainly have most of this already.

## Ingredients

- **Short-grain rice** — 1 kg. The stickier the better.
- **Nuruk** — about 100–150 g. This is the one thing you'll likely order online.
- **Water** — filtered or dechlorinated, roughly 1.2 L.

## Equipment

- A wide **glass or food-grade plastic jar**, at least 4 L, with a loose lid.
- A **big pot** and a **steamer basket** (or a colander that fits).
- A clean **spoon** long enough to reach the bottom.
- A **warm spot** that stays around 22–26 °C (72–79 °F).

## Before you begin

1. **Sanitize everything** the rice or must will touch. Boiling water works.
2. **Rinse the rice** until the water runs clear, then soak it for an hour.
3. Clear your calendar for a quick daily stir over the next week.
