If you have only ever met makgeolli as a cold, milky drink in a green bottle,
you have met the shy, factory version. Home-brewed makgeolli is a different
animal: alive, faintly fizzy, and somewhere between sweet, sour, and savory.

## The whole recipe, in one sentence

Steamed rice, water, and **nuruk** are combined and left in a warm place; the
wild molds and yeasts in the nuruk break the rice down into sugar and then into
alcohol. That's it. Everything else is refinement.

## Why it's a great first brew

- **Forgiving.** Makgeolli is meant to be cloudy and rustic, so small mistakes
  rarely ruin a batch.
- **Fast.** You can be drinking a young makgeolli in about a week.
- **Cheap.** Rice, water, and a bag of nuruk cost very little.

> Brewing is mostly waiting and tasting. The more batches you make, the more
> your tongue learns what "ready" feels like.
